About the Founder

Hi, I am Soumya Prashant Bhandare (Krishna) — a 15-year-old Independent Tech Founder, Software Architect, and Robotics & Product Designer based in Maharashtra, India.

My journey into the world of computer science did not follow the conventional classroom route; it started with a pure passion for breaking down complex systems and understanding how software interacts with hardware at the lowest levels. Today, I am the creator and core developer of KIDS (Krishna Innovation Driven System), a high-performance, bilingual programming language engineered from scratch to make low-level programming systems both accessible and robust.

The Philosophy Behind My Work

Growing up in an educational and creative household—with my father serving as a dedicated Zilla Parishad educator and an accomplished Marathi poet (author of the book "Kavdsa"), I learned early on that true innovation lies at the intersection of structure and expression. This inspired the bilingual nature of KIDS, bridging native linguistics with complex machine logic to give developers a unique way to express code.

Technical Focus & Vision

I specialize in low-level systems engineering, memory management design, and compiler construction. After stabilizing the execution stack and custom bytecode Virtual Machine (VM) for KIDS v0.3, my current research base in Chandrapur is dedicated to building KIDS v0.4 — rewriting the entire compiler infrastructure in Bare-Metal Assembly Language for native, direct-chip interaction in advanced robotics.

When I am not writing lexers or optimization pipelines, I blend the principles of Engineering Drawing with physical product modeling to sketch and design the structural blueprints of custom robot modules that will eventually be powered by the KIDS ecosystem.

Driven by logic. Guided by creativity. Building the future of hardware-software integration.
